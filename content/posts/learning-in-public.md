Writing in public adds a productive constraint: it asks an idea to become
clear enough that another person can examine it.

That does not mean pretending to be certain. In fact, the most useful public
notes make their uncertainty visible. They distinguish observation from
interpretation and interpretation from speculation.

## Explain the edge of your understanding

When I can repeat a definition but cannot generate an example, my
understanding is fragile. When I can use an idea but cannot explain its
limits, it is incomplete.

Writing exposes these edges. A blank paragraph is often evidence of a missing
connection rather than a lack of vocabulary.

## Make claims easy to inspect

Good technical notes show their working:

```text
claim → evidence → interpretation → open question
```

This shape makes an essay more useful even when its conclusion changes. A
reader can disagree with the interpretation while still benefiting from the
evidence and the question.

## Publish versions, not verdicts

Treating every post as a final declaration creates unnecessary pressure.
Treating it as a version encourages revision. Add a date, keep a changelog
when the changes matter, and correct mistakes directly.

The point of learning in public is not to always be right. It is to make the
process of becoming less wrong more rigorous and more generous.
